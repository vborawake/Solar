Ground Mount Solar for your Home

When homeowners think of adding a solar energy system, rooftop solar is probably the first to come to mind. But ground-mounted solar panels (like the ones you've seen at solar farms and solar carports) are just as effective. We cover the top five things you need to know about ground-mounted solar panels.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

  • Ground-mounted solar panels operate like a typical rooftop system but are generally more efficient.
  • Ground-mounted solar panel installations are usually more expensive than roof-mounted systems but can maximize your renewable energy production depending on your roof's orientation and shading.
  • Standard ground and pole mounts are the two most common types of ground-mounted solar system installations.
  • Your available space, the system cost, and electricity consumption are all important considerations when deciding between a roof-mounted and ground-mounted solar installation.

Ground Mount Solar Panel
Systems Offer Many Benefits

There are several reasons to choose a rooftop solar system, and a few of the top reasons are listed here:

KEY TAKEAWAYS

  • Ground-mounted solar panel systems can be located on open land and don't require drilling into your roof. Additionally, ground-mounted solar panels can be set at the perfect angle to optimize energy production, whereas rooftop solar arrays are dependent on the pitch and tilt of your roof. Thus, ground-mount solar can generate more electricity (and save you more on your electric bill) than a similarly sized rooftop system.
  • Gound-mounted solar panels are also easily accessible for cleaning and maintenance. If you live in an area that receives a lot of snow in the winter, being able to sweep the snow off your solar panels quickly is a major convenience.
  • Though more expensive, standard ground-mounted solar costs are comparable to a rooftop solar energy system installation. Systems with trackers come with additional upfront costs, but the resulting increase in electricity production can make your installation more cost-effective.
